About

Who I am as a designer

My work usually starts with listening—understanding what people are trying to do, what’s frustrating them, and where a product can quietly support them.

I’m especially drawn to projects around wellness, education, and everyday tools. I like exploring how small design decisions can change how someone feels using a product.

On the technical side, I’m comfortable moving between research, UX flows, and front-end implementation. This site is part of that growth: a cleaner, more focused version of my earlier portfolio, built to highlight clarity and craft.

How I like to work

Explore

Start with questions, not assumptions. I gather context through conversations, existing data, and quick audits.

Shape

Turn insights into flows, sketches, and prototypes. I share early and invite feedback often.

Refine

Polish the details: hierarchy, states, microcopy, and handoff notes that make implementation smoother.
